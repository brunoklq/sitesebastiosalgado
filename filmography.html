"THE SALT
OF THE EARTH"

Documentary about and with Salgado, directed by Wim Wenders and Salgado's son Juliano Ribeiro Salgado.

The documentary chronicles Salgado's expansive body of work, which spans decades and encompasses a wide range of social, environmental, and humanitarian issues. Through interviews, personal anecdotes, and stunning visuals of his photographs, viewers are invited to explore Salgado's deep empathy for humanity and his unwavering commitment to shedding light on the struggles and triumphs of people around the world.

One of the film's central themes is Salgado's transformative experiences as a witness to some of the most profound moments in recent history, including conflicts, famines, and mass migrations. His photographs capture not only the harsh realities of human suffering but also the resilience, dignity, and beauty that endure amidst adversity.

"The Salt of the Earth" also delves into Salgado's personal life, including his relationship with his wife, Lélia Wanick Salgado, who played a pivotal role in supporting and shaping his career. Together, they founded the Instituto Terra, a nonprofit organization dedicated to environmental conservation and reforestation in Brazil, reflecting Salgado's deep connection to the land and his commitment to preserving it for future generations.

Through its powerful storytelling and breathtaking imagery, "The Salt of the Earth" invites viewers to contemplate the profound impact of Salgado's work and to reflect on the interconnectedness of humanity and the natural world. It serves as a tribute to Salgado's extraordinary talent, compassion, and humanity, reminding us of the enduring power of art to inspire, educate, and provoke change.
